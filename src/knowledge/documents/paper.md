# Paper Waste — Disposal & Handling Guide

## Clean Paper and Office Paper

Clean office paper, printer paper, and writing paper are highly recyclable. Paper fibers can be recycled 5–7 times before they become too short to bond into new paper. It is one of the most recovered materials globally. CleanBot action: collect_recyclable. Bin: blue. Priority: high.

## Cardboard and Corrugated Boxes

Corrugated cardboard and flat cardboard are widely recycled. They should be flattened before placing in the recycling bin. Wet or heavily soiled cardboard loses structural integrity and contaminates recycling loads. Wet cardboard found outdoors: CleanBot action collect_landfill. Dry cardboard: collect_recyclable. Bin: blue (dry) / black (wet). Priority: high.

## Newspapers and Magazines

Newsprint and magazine paper are recyclable. Glossy magazine pages are acceptable in most recycling programs as the coating is water-soluble in the pulping process. CleanBot action: collect_recyclable. Bin: blue. Priority: medium.

## Paper Bags

Paper bags without plastic handles or coatings are recyclable. Paper bags with plastic liners, wax coatings, or plastic handles cannot be recycled as paper — they must be separated by material type. CleanBot action: collect_recyclable (plain paper bag). Bin: blue. Priority: medium.

## Paper Cups

Paper cups for hot beverages (coffee cups) are typically lined with a thin plastic or wax film to prevent leakage. This lining makes most paper cups non-recyclable in standard streams. Some municipalities have specialized cup-recycling programs. Do not place in standard paper recycling. CleanBot action: collect_landfill. Bin: black. Priority: medium.

## Pizza Boxes and Food-Contaminated Cardboard

Greasy pizza boxes and food-saturated cardboard cannot be recycled — oil and grease prevent paper fibers from bonding during pulping and contaminate entire recycling loads. They can be composted if the municipality accepts contaminated cardboard. CleanBot action: collect_compost if compost available, otherwise collect_landfill. Bin: green (compost) / black (landfill). Priority: medium.

## Paper Straws

Paper straws are compostable and often industrially compostable. They break down in 2–6 weeks in a compost environment. CleanBot action: collect_compost. Bin: green. Priority: low.

## Receipts (Thermal Paper)

Cash register receipts are printed on thermal paper coated with BPA (bisphenol A) or BPS, which are endocrine-disrupting chemicals. Thermal paper receipts should not be recycled as they contaminate paper pulp. CleanBot action: collect_landfill. Bin: black. Hazard: low (skin contact), medium (water contamination if landfilled improperly).

## Paper Towels and Tissues

Paper towels and facial tissues are made from short, already-recycled fibers. They are not recyclable but are compostable. Used food-contaminated paper towels are compostable. CleanBot action: collect_compost. Bin: green. Priority: low.

## Wet Paper

Wet paper loses its fiber structure and has no recycling value. It should go to landfill unless compostable. CleanBot should not attempt to collect heavily saturated paper as it may clog collection mechanisms.
